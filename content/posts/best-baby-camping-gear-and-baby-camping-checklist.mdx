## What Is The Best Age To Take A Baby Camping?

Each baby is different so it is hard to define the best age. A newborn who has not yet gotten in a pattern of sleep during the night would be more challenging to camp with than a baby who can sleep stretches during the night.

Your baby doesn’t have to be sleeping through the night to go camping. My youngest was not when I started taking her and she does fine. 

![baby and toddler camping trip](https://res.cloudinary.com/ds8uw7t44/image/upload/v1685975339/best-age-to-take-baby-camping.jpg)

A baby who wakes once or twice is easier to take camping. For me that has always been once my babies are 4 months plus. At this stage of my baby's life I have established bedtime routines making nighttime easier when camping.

A sweet spot to start could be **before your baby is crawling**. Once a baby is crawling it becomes harder to keep him/her to one spot. 

Didn't start when yours was a baby, check out **[Best Toddler Camping Gear](/best-toddler-camping-gear-and-toddler-camping-checklist/)** instead.

## Baby Camping Gear Related to Feeding

Camping with a baby requires preparation. One important aspect of it is ensuring your baby will be fed. Now, if your baby has not started solid foods yet the gear will be minimal; especially if you baby is breastfed. 

However, once your baby starts solids you will want to bring the right camping essentials for your baby to eat outdoors.

## Baby Camping Gear for Sleep

Taking a baby camping means figuring out where your baby will sleep. For me this was where I spent most of my time researching, I wanted to be certain my baby was set up to sleep as best as she could when camping.

You have a couple options for a baby camping bed. The most important factor is that it is safe. American Academy of Pediatrics [**guidelines**](https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/safe-sleep/) are:

- \-Baby to be placed on their back in their own space

- \-Use crib, bassinet or portable playard with a firm mattress

- \-Nothing in the crib except baby, no loose items

After considering the safety guidelines and knowing my large family tent I choose to camp with my babies in a travel crib. It was important for me that my baby has her own space that she is familiar with.

## Baby Camping Gear: Diaper Essentials

It is important to consider how you will change diapers when camping with a baby. A baby needs to be changed frequently so best to come with a plan. And I can't promise you won't have any **[baby diaper blowouts](/baby-diaper-blowout-tricks-to-help-you-manage-the-situation/)** while camping.

### **Portable changing mat**

![baby camping gear portable changing pad](https://res.cloudinary.com/ds8uw7t44/image/upload/v1685975328/diaper-blowout.jpg)

A **[small portable changing mat](https://www.amazon.com/Portable-Changing-Diaper-Station-Compact/dp/B07D4P9F5P?crid=ACH9W9R21FPF&keywords=travel%2Bchanging%2Bpad%2Bfor%2Bbaby&qid=1684665325&sprefix=travel%2Bchanging%2Bpad%2Bfor%2Bbaby%2Caps%2C79&sr=8-8&th=1&linkCode=ll1&tag=allaboutmom06-20&linkId=9e3d0a85f4bd9e56be7ab9abfbde50ca&language=en_US&ref_=as_li_ss_tl)** can be used anywhere. Camping with a baby means collecting all dirty diapers and stashing them till you leave. Dirty diapers smell and you will not want to keep smelling them after the fact. Bring a roll of **[odor free poop bags](https://www.amazon.com/Munchkin-Hammer-Diaper-Refills-Count/dp/B009F98SSS?crid=OBZ5RA4151YD&keywords=baby+poop+bags&qid=1684665360&sprefix=baby+poop+bags%2Caps%2C89&sr=8-6&linkCode=ll1&tag=allaboutmom06-20&linkId=f3aa146822c51091e7d1ecf92641883f&language=en_US&ref_=as_li_ss_tl)** to seal in all the diapers till you can properly dispose of them.
